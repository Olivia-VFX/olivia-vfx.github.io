🌱 Shakespeare Programming Language

🌱 Seedling 📅 June 2026

❓ The Question

Can programming become less intimidating by using storytelling and a language closer to conversational English?

💭 Motivation

While taking Computer Science at school, I've become increasingly interested in how programming is introduced to children. Many educational tools focus on puzzles or abstract challenges, but I wonder whether storytelling could be more welcoming. This will explore whether programming can be taught through directing and creating a Shakespeare play using a language this is between conversational English and traditional code.

🌱 Current Thoughts

Maybe base the play on A Midsummers Nights Dream, allowing kids to design the characters costumes, direct their movement, choose dialogue (Easter Eggs??) and introduce them into the very basics of programming syntax.

Challenges

Compiling the language from entered 'programming' into Python/C# etc, identify bugs in the language, create interesting and fun lessons, design the language with 8-12yr olds in mind.
