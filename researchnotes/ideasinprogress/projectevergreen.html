Project Evergreen

Exploring whether thoughtful design can make subjects like programming feel more approachable to children from a wider range of backgrounds and interests

❓ The Question

Can technology help children grow in curiosity, confidence, and understanding without relying on the attention-grabbing techniques that dominate many digital experiences?

🌍 Why This Matters

While researching edcuational inequality, I became interested in the idea that the greatest opportunity to reduce educational inequality is during primary school. At the same time, many digital experiences designed for children optimise for attention rather than understanding. Project Evergreen explores whether technology can instead encourage curiosity, creativity, and long-term learning.

📖 What I'm Reading

  • Cognitive psychology
  • Developmental psychology
  • Educational research
  • Game design
  • Human-computer interaction
  • Accessibility
  • Child development

🎮 Design Principles

  • 🌱 Curiosity before rewards
  • 🌿 Exploration before optimisation
  • 🍃 Understanding before memorisation
  • 🎨 Creativity before competition
  • 🌍 Accessible to as many children as possible
  • 💻 Technology should support learning, not replace it

🧠 Ideas & Experiments

Current ideas include story-driven mathematics adventures, interactive Shakespeare plays that teach programming, collaborative science explorations, and creative design challenges where children learn by building, experimenting, and discovering rather than following fixed instructions. Across all of these ideas, the aim is to create experiences that are calm, welcoming, and rewarding because they foster understanding—not because they maximise engagement.

⚠️ Challenges

  • Designing experiences that remain engaging without relying on addictive reward systems
  • Create stories which prioritise understanding
  • Make an easy to understand curriculum which can be used by anyone
  • Creating stories that naturally adapt to different learning speeds and backgrounds

🌱 Next Steps

  • Read books on child education and psychology
  • Begin to brainstorm stories which are engaging but not addictive, unique and enjoyable
  • Begin to understand what makes current primary education tools addictive and alternative methods
  • Understand what makes children feel welcome and can help people from a range of backgrounds
  • Read existing educational games critically and analyse what they do well and where they fall short

🌳 Evergreen Philosophy

I don't believe technology shoudl replace teachers, books, or imagination. Instead, I hope it can become another tool that helps children ask questions, experiment freely, and discovere ideas they might never have encountered otherwise. Project Evergreen is less about building games, and more about exploring how technoogy can nuture curiosity that lasts beyond the screen.
