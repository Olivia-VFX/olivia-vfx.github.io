Vectors are mathematical values which have a direction and a magnitude (a value such as 4m). An example of a vector is 3m East, where East is the direction and 3m is the magnitude, these simple values and direction are the basis for many complex computer graphics and physics models.

They are typically represented graphically by an arrow where length represents direction and arrowhead shows direction, the starting point is called the tail and the endpoint is called the head. Algebraically, vectors are are shows in component form, such as v→=⟨vx,vy,vz⟩ in a three-dimensional space, where each compentent represents the projection in the different axis.

Typically vectors are mapped in a space called Euclidean vector space (a space in geometry intended to represent physical space).
