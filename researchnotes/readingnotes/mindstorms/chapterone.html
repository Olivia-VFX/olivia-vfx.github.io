Chapter 1: Computers and Computer Cultures

🌱 Reading Notes 📖 Chapter: Chapter 1 📅 July 2026 🌳 Foundational Atlas #003.02

Before Reading

Before reading the first chapter, I wasn't aware of the impact that the culture has on children, Papert emphasises the importance of active and self-directed earning, and how computers can concretise and personalise formal thinking. Papert explores this through programming and how it fixes the flaws of the current education system

Summary

Papert explores the impact of cultural influence on kids perspectives of subjects, and highlights the importance of learning being active and self-directed suggesting that computers can help concretise and personalise information. Papert suggests that programming can help teach kids isolate problems and reduce the fear of failure. By thinking mechanically it helps kids think step-by-step and be more confident in approaching a problem, fixing problems that the current education system flaws, but highlights how theres no certainty it will work.

Key Ideas

  • Kids shouldn't be influenced by external cultural influences
  • Computers can help to concretise and personalise information
  • Programming can help teach kids to isolate problems and reduce their fear of failure
  • Mechanical thinking can help to fix some of the current education systems flaws

Questions Raised

  • Can teaching that is built by the learner be applied to a story based education system?
  • Why does combinatorial thinking develop later in life

New Words/Phrases

  • Epistemologist
  • Concrete and formal thinking
  • Combinatorial thinking
  • Self-referential thinking

Reflection

Before the chapter, I didn't fully understand or appreciate the impact that cultural inflences have on children, and wasn't aware of the ways in which programming/mechanical thinking can help children develop and grow. Paperts emphasises on the need to modernise education and that reminded me of Project Evergreen and how it may take a step in the right direction to modernise education.
