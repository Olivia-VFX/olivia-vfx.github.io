Introduction: Computers for Children

🌱 Reading Notes 📖 Chapter: Introduction 📅 July 2026 🌳 Foundational Atlas #003.01

Before Reading

Before reading the introduction, I thought the book would focus on common teaching practices, but instead, Papert strays from the norm and explores more unconventional ways of educating kids

Summary

Papert explores the dangers of kids having prejudiced views of their own intelligence and subjects, suggesting that using Paigetian learning can both make education more efficient and improve understanding. He uses the Turtle to show how programming can be taught through interactive robots.

Key Ideas

  • Kids shouldn't have prejudiced views of their own intelligence and subjects
  • Children can learn through doing
  • Kids can learn things without being taught

Questions Raised

  • Can Piagetion learning be turned into a game/story?
  • How can we reduce the insecurities around a childs own view of their intelligence?
  • How can we use the fact that programming changes kids views of maths and apply it to other subjects?

New Words/Phrases

  • Piagetian Learning
  • LOGO
  • Microworlds

Reflection

Before the chapter, I though that the concepts explored would be an exploration of concepts typically explored but Papert discusses concepts from Paigetian Learning and the strictness of current education setups. This exploration aligns well with my Project Evergreen philosophy and desires for the project
