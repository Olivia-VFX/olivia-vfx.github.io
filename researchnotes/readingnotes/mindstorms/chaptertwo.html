Chapter 2: Mathophobia: the Fear of Learning

🌱 Reading Notes 📖 Chapter: Chapter 2 📅 July 2026 🌳 Foundational Atlas #003.03

Before Reading

Before reading the first chapter, I wasn't aware of the full impact that societies traditional divide between humanities and STEM subject has on children, as well as the impact that perceived deficiencies have on peoples relationships to subjects, and inturn childrens relationships with subjects. I also wasnt aware of how outdated education techniques could be modified to be more appropiable to students

Summary

Papert explores the impact of societal prejudices and divides of subjects, emphasising the impact that those divides and societal curated 'deficiencies' have on childrens relationships with subjects and areas of education. Papert explores how computers can challenge assumptions of subjects and modernise subjects, such as maths, where current curriculums are outdated and limiting how successful students could be in it. Papert also introduces Turtle Geometry and its principles as an alternative to the current 'school maths'.

Key Ideas

  • Kids should be taught by people who enjoy and appreciate the subject
  • Computers can help to challenge assumptions
  • A positive educational environment can help to improve education
  • School maths is outdated and based on historical conditions that are now obsolete, meaning it is enjoyed less
  • A truly successful education system should be based on continuity, empowerment and cultural resonance

Questions Raised

  • How can failure be redirected and used to empower growth and development?
  • Are tests and quizzes the best way of giving kids a positive view of their aptitude?
  • How can we exploit computers to provide links between subjects and day-to-day life?
  • Why is the most personal knowledge often the most mathematical?
  • How we apply the Turtle Geometry Principles to a storybased education system?

New Words/Phrases

  • Piagetian conservations
  • Cognitive repression
  • Pedagogy
  • Cognitive competence

Reflection

Papert raised valid concerns about societies views of subjects and our own aptitude, highlighting the significant impact it has on childrens development, raising the question of how to reduce that impact and what will happen to both kids and adults if we change our own views of aptitude. He also reflected on 'school maths' which is still being used today, despite many decades of advanced computers, which made me question if our current maths curriculum is best for development and success, or is just another way people are scared of modernisation.
