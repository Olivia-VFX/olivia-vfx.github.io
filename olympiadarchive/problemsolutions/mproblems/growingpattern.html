#001 🌱 A Growing Pattern

Source: Atlas Challenge Difficulty: 🌱 Beginner Topics: Patterns • Sequences • Proof Status: Solved

❓ Problem

The numbers
1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21 ...
continue in the same pattern.
What is the 10th number in the sequence? And why does the pattern work?

💭 First Thoughts

I first noticed the numbers were increasing by a changing constant, first by 2 then 3 then 4 etc, the amount being added was increasing.
That made me wonder whether the sequence was quadratic.

🔍 Finding a Pattern

1
1 + 2 = 3 [2nd term]
3 + 3 = 6 [3rd term]
6 + 4 = 10 [4th term]
10 + 5 = 15 [5th term]
Each new term is created by adding the next consecutive integer

My First Observation

The second differences were all equal to 1, which suggested that the sequence may be quadratic, this suggested that a formula involving n^2 probably existed. But, this told me what kind of forumla but not why the sequence behaved this way.

Looking deeper, I realise each term was created by adding the next integer. This shifted my perspective from algebra to geometry, revealing the numbers are triangular numbers.

✨ Finding a Formula

This problem introduced me to Gauss's proof which I had never seen before: 1+2+...+n = [n(n+1)] / 2

This inspired me to explore this proof in a article/page in the future!

🌍 Why Does This Matter?

Triangular numbers appear often throughout maths, the count handshakes between people, appear in graph theory,describe combinations, and arise naturally in algorithms. This connects many different areas of maths

🌱 What I learned

This problem reminded me that maths begins by noticing patterns, and sometimes meeting a new proof in the process
